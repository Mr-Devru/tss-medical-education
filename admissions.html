FAQ Help Desk

Admissions FAQs

Find quick, sensible answers to common questions asked by prospective students and parents.

Can Arts or Commerce students apply for BHA?

Yes, absolutely! The Bachelors in Hospital Administration (BHA) program at our RGUHS-affiliated degree college is uniquely designed to welcome students from any academic stream—Arts, Commerce, or Science in 12th standard. Most medical allied science courses require a Science background, making BHA an exceptionally popular choice for non-science students aiming to enter the growing healthcare corporate sector.

Is residential hostel accommodation available?

Yes, we offer clean, comfortable, and highly secure separate residential hostels for boys and girls on/near our Sirsi campus. Hostels are monitored by strict wardens and 24/7 security personnel. Nutritious, hygienic vegetarian meals are served daily in the dining hall.

What is the duration of BSc MLT degree course?

BSc MLT (Bachelor of Science in Medical Laboratory Technology) is structured as a 3-year academic study program plus 1 full year of clinical internship. The 1-year internship is completed at our parent hospital in Sirsi, providing critical diagnostic experience on active pathology equipment before graduation.

Do you have a parent hospital for practical rotations?

Yes! Our parent hospital, the TSS Shripad Hegde Kadave Institute of Medical Sciences, is a fully functional multi-specialty medical hospital in Sirsi. Unlike other colleges, all our degree and diploma students get real bedside and laboratory clinical exposure starting from their very first year of study, which is an invaluable professional advantage.

Are the degrees and diplomas recognised nationally?

Yes, absolutely. Our degree courses are affiliated to the Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences (RGUHS), Bengaluru—a top tier, nationally recognised health sciences university. Our diploma courses are fully recognised by the Karnataka Nursing and Paramedical Sciences Education Authority (KNPSEA), Bengaluru. Graduates are eligible for government jobs and private sector placements nationwide.

Can I get a job abroad after completing these courses?

Yes. Healthcare professionals trained under RGUHS degree guidelines are in massive demand globally, particularly in the United Kingdom, Ireland, GCC/Gulf countries, and Canada. Since clinical training is done in our actual multi-specialty hospital, students gain the solid, practical laboratory and radiological experience needed to pass international medical licensing assessments easily.

What documents are mandatory at the time of admission?

Mandatory documents include: Original 10th marksheet, Original 12th marksheet (PCMB Science for BSc, any stream for BHA), Transfer Certificate (TC), Migration Certificate (if coming from outside Karnataka boards), Caste/Income Certificates (if seeking SC/ST/OBC concessions), Aadhar card copies, and 5 recent passport-size photos.

Is there any separate entrance examination for seats?

No separate entrance exam is required. Admissions to both our colleges are conducted on a direct merit basis depending on the marks obtained in your 10th or 12th qualifying examinations. Early registration is advised as seats are strictly limited per course to maintain high-quality teacher-to-student ratios inside our hospital labs.

When do clinical classes and hospital sessions start?

The academic sessions generally start around September-October, in accordance with the official calendar timelines released by RGUHS University and the KNPSEA Authority. Registered students are notified of the exact date via call and post well in advance.

What is the duration of paramedical diplomas post 10th standard?

Under KNPSEA guidelines, students enrolling immediately after 10th standard will study for 3 years to earn their professional diploma (DMLT, DMIT, or DOT & AT). However, students who join after completing 12th standard Science (PCMB) study on an accelerated 2-year path to earn the exact same diploma.
